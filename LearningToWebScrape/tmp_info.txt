               <p class="review_comment description ieSucks">I'm reviewing the Saturday brunch...no comment on the lunch offerings, the sandwiches, etc.<br /><br />I'm an 82-year old living in a 28-year old's body. I hate standing in a line of my peers for weekend brunch. Granted, Cafe Luna is one of the few weekend brunches in Cambridge-Somerville that I might consider standing in line for, but even then, nope, not gonna do it. I'm grumpy, I'm impatient, and I'm tired. My work schedule gives me certain weekday mornings off, so being out and about among the hustle of weekend brunch humanity makes me especially angry. I'm always comparing it (unfavorably) to the happy lull of a quiet weekday morning breakfast. Stand in line for weekend brunch? No way. That's just me.<br /><br />So here's the deal: my sister-in-law wanted to take me out for my birthday last month, and she wanted to go to Saturday brunch in Cambridge. What does she do? She calls Cafe Luna and makes RESERVATION. What? A weekend brunch that takes reservation? Yes. We arrived five minutes early with one member of our party missing. There was a line out the door in the bitter cold - typical weekend brunch in Cambridge. Someone who must have been the manager was walking around greeting people, smiling, reassuring potential customers but forcefully enforcing the rules and logic of the line (I appreciate that). He told us we'd have to wait for the missing member of our party to arrive; she did arrive a few minutes late, and we were promptly seated. I was amazed. A weekend brunch that takes reservations, treats its customers humanely, and does it all efficiently. <br /><br />That alone wouldn't earn Cafe Luna five stars. The brunch was incredible. I ordered &quot;everything but the kitchen sink,&quot; a menu item that allowed me to sample all their meats, their lovely citrusy hollandaise sauce (I love it when you can really taste the citrus), an egg, and a waffle. The corned beef hash was out of sight. The ham was perfect (I'm a ham snob). The waffle blew my mind. This particular dish didn't feature maple syrup, but the waffle was sweet enough on its own. My wife ordered the eggs benedict, which I tried and found delicious. I absolutely loved every part of this breakfast...and I'm not normally enthusiastic about breakfast fare. (One of the reasons I refuse to stand in line for brunch is that, normally, I don't like brunch all that much!) <br /><br />One complaint: our waiter, though excellent, didn't offer any coffee refills. The restaurant was PACKED, and although we felt comfortable and not crowded in, I think it affected the service. I'd generally deduct a star for an empty coffee mug, but everything else about Cafe Luna deserves five stars. What a great experience! I'm coming back.</p>

         <h4>Specialties</h4> 
         <div class="inner">
          <p>Cafe Luna specializes in panini and specialty sandwiches, &nbsp;large salads and gourmet flatbread pizza using the freshest ingredients. We have a hand picked and affordable wine list, use only Italian espresso beans for our espresso drinks, and have a good selection of sweets and treats from local artisan bakeries. Thursday through Sunday afternoon, we feature live music. <br />On the week end, we are probably most famous for our brunch. The menu includes a &quot;French Toast Fantasy&quot; with things like S'Mores, Nutella Banana, Lemon Blueberry and for the Fall, Pumpkin Pie and Peanut Butter Cup. We also have real corned beef hash and eggs benedict with ham, salmon or lobster. We are small, so reservations for brunch are recommended.</p>
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="section"> 
         <h4>History</h4> 
         <div class="inner"> 
          <p><em>Established in 2007</em></p> 
          <p>The owners, Matt and Teri Haymer, have always appreciated good food and had a desire to open a place where hospitality &nbsp;and quality were presented in an affordable, welcoming atmosphere. Teri had some culinary training in San Francisco and Italy but chose a path in the corporate world until the time was right to pursue her real passion, cooking, food and wine. This cafe has exceeded their expectations and they hope to continue to grow it and bring new and exciting food and wine to Central Square for years to come.</p>

          <div class="biz-bio"> 
           <p>As a life long cook and foodie, I made the decision three years ago to leave corporate life and open a cafe that would provide superior customer service and top quality food in a casual, neighborhood establishment. A true family business, Matt is the front of the house during Brunch, and my son Alex is the General Manager and one of the cooks and my younger son Justin helps on the week ends. We are joined by a host of enthusiastic and talented employees.</p>
